# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Travelers planning trips to Puerto Rico — tourists who want to discover the island beyond guidebooks, and locals looking for weekend ideas, staycations, and spots they've never been. They're on their phone, probably in a moment of intention ("where should I go this weekend?"), and they're skeptical of generic recommendations that could apply to any destination.

Secondary: Puerto Rican locals and content creators who want to contribute and be featured on the map.

## Product Purpose

PaDonde is Puerto Rico's first AI travel companion — a mobile app that builds personalized road trips, surfaces beach conditions, recommends local food and hidden gems, and aggregates real picks from Puerto Rican creators. The landing page exists to capture waitlist signups before launch and establish PaDonde as the definitive local-knowledge source for exploring Puerto Rico.

Success = qualified email signups from people who feel the island's pull through the page, not just another app announcement.

## Brand Personality

Intimate, intelligent, proud.

The voice of a 17-year-old who grew up in Guaynabo and knows every beach, every hidden spot, and exactly which tourism recommendations are fake. Speaks Spanish and English with equal ease. Not trying to be Apple or Airbnb — has its own thing.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI startup aesthetic**: purple-to-blue gradients, "Introducing [Product]" hero copy, glassmorphism cards, SaaS-cream color palette. The look of Y-Combinator demo day.
- **Stock-photo tourism**: "Discover Paradise" headlines, smiling tourists holding cameras, images that could be any tropical island. Manufactured, not earned.
- **TripAdvisor / Yelp / Google Maps**: data-dense, review-first, zero personality. Designed to hold information, not to inspire.

## Design Principles

1. **Puerto Rican voice first.** Every design decision should feel like it comes from inside the island, not from a mainland tech company looking at Puerto Rico. Typography, palette, motion — all earned from the place, not applied to it.

2. **Intelligence as warmth.** The AI features should feel like a knowledgeable local friend, not a chatbot. Restraint and specificity signal trust more than feature lists.

3. **Show the place, not the product.** Let Puerto Rico be the hero. Resist the urge to explain every feature. The visual atmosphere sells the app; the copy closes.

4. **Earned detail.** Typography and layout carry the weight. Reserve decoration for moments that matter. Don't ornament every surface.

5. **Proud, not loud.** Confidence through editorial calm, not hype-tech energy. The opposite of a Product Hunt launch post.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 4.5:1 contrast on all body text, keyboard navigable, visible focus indicators. `prefers-reduced-motion` already respected site-wide (all GSAP animations gate on it). Bilingual by design — app serves both English and Spanish speakers equally.
